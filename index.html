Experiments with deep learning and transfer learning using Google Inception model

Posted on Fri 01 April 2016 in dev

Google recently released a trained model (Inception) which they used to achieve excellent performance on the ImageNet challenge. Training such a model takes a significant amount of time and resources. I was curious whether their model could be used for purposes other than yielding the 1000 image categories used by the challenge.

A little bit of reading suggests that this should be possible through the principle of transfer learning. The last layer of the network in their model outputs a category label. If we instead take the output of the second to last layer in their network, we get feature vectors- an abstract, compact representation of the input image. The task I decided to explore using these feature vectors is simple image similarity.

To perform an image similarity experiment, I modified google's classify_image.py to write feature vectors into pickled numpy arrays for fast loading later. I then computed feature vectors for 100 images of one type (images of one person's EKG, for example) and feature vectors for 100 images of a second type (images of another person's EKG). If I compute the pairwise euclidean distances between the feature vectors for all these images, it's clear that the feature vectors will separate images of the two classes, as shown below. In other words, images of type 1 are usually "closer/more similar" to other images of class 1 than they are to images of class 2.

Useful vim keyboard remapping for Mac OSX

Posted on Fri 01 April 2016 in misc

If your a vim user, one of your most frequently used keys is probably Esc, to exit editing mode. One of your least frequently used keys in general is probably the Caps-Lock key, so why not swap Esc and Caps-Lock?

Seil https://pqrs.org/osx/karabiner/seil.html.en allows you to do just that. Once you've remapped the Esc key as shown in the screenshot below, if you haven't already, add:

set -o vi

to your .bashrc and now you can easily edit all your bash commands using the vim keybindings you're accustomed to.
